climate change will cause direct damage to agriculture

Source: link

Take crops as an example.Crops grown in the United States are vital to the food supply here and around the world.Changes in temperature, atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2), and the frequency and intensity of extreme weather can all have a significant impact on crop yields

For any given crop, the impact of rising temperatures will depend on the optimal temperature for growing and reproducing crops. In some areas, a warmer climate could favor the types of crops typically grown locally, or allow farmers to switch to crops currently grown in warmer areas.Conversely, if higher temperatures exceed the optimum temperature for crops, yields will decline
